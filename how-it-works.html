How It Works

Understanding price per unit and how to use this calculator to shop smarter.

1

Choose your measure type

Select how the products are measured — by weight (g, kg), volume (ml, L), piece count (tablets, sheets), or area (m²). Pick the one that matches what's printed on the packaging.

Example: Laundry detergent → Volume (ml or L). Paracetamol → Count (tablets). Cheese → Weight (g).
2

Select your unit and currency

Pick the specific unit shown on the pack, and set the currency you're shopping in. The calculator supports SGD, USD, MYR, AUD, GBP, EUR, JPY, and HKD.

Tip: Keep units consistent across all products you're comparing — e.g. all in ml, not mixing ml and L.
3

Enter each product's details

For each brand or pack, enter the brand name (and optionally a description like "1L carton"), the shelf price, and the quantity or size. Add as many products as you need.

The description field is optional — use it to tell apart different pack sizes of the same brand (e.g. "500ml" vs "1L bottle").
4

Click Compare

Hit the "Compare →" button. The calculator divides the price by the quantity for each product and ranks them from cheapest to most expensive per unit.

5

Read your results

The best-value product is highlighted in green. The priciest is flagged in red. You'll also see a verdict summarising how much you save by choosing the best option.

Use the sort dropdown in the results to re-order by cheapest, priciest, or name.

The Formula

Price Per Unit = Total Price ÷ Pack Quantity

That's it. Simple division — but supermarkets rely on the fact that most shoppers don't do this mental maths while rushing through the aisle. A 1L bottle for $3.20 looks cheaper than a 500ml bottle for $1.80, but the per-unit calculation tells you otherwise.

This tool does the maths instantly across all the brands you enter, so you can make the right call in seconds.

A Real-World Example

Here's a typical comparison for orange juice at a supermarket:

Brand Pack size Price Price / ml
Brand A (small) 250 ml S$1.50 S$0.0060
Brand B (medium) 1,000 ml S$3.80 S$0.0038
Brand C (large) 2,000 ml S$6.50 S$0.0033 Best value

Brand C looks expensive at $6.50 — but it's actually 45% cheaper per ml than the small pack from Brand A.
