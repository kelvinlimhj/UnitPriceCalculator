Smart Shopping Tips

10 practical habits that will save you money every time you shop — without clipping a single coupon.

01

Never judge by shelf price alone

A S$2.50 product is not necessarily cheaper than a S$4.00 one. The only number that matters is cost per unit — per gram, ml, or piece. Train yourself to always divide the price by the quantity before you decide.

02

Bigger packs are not always better value

Supermarkets sometimes price smaller packs more competitively when they're on promotion. Always check the per-unit cost of every available size — the 500g pack on offer might beat the "economy" 2kg pack.

Use PriceCheck to compare all available sizes of the same product before buying the big one.
03

Look above and below eye level

Supermarkets pay premium fees to place products at eye level — those are typically not the best deals. The store brands and budget options are usually on the bottom shelves or the top shelf. Always scan the full shelving unit.

04

Store brands vs name brands — do the maths

Own-brand or house-brand products are often manufactured in the same factories as premium brands. The per-unit price can be 20–50% lower. Run a comparison before assuming the name brand is worth the premium.

05

Compare across supermarkets before a big shop

Prices for the same product can vary significantly between NTUC FairPrice, Cold Storage, Giant, and Sheng Siong. For staples you buy regularly, a quick comparison before a big monthly shop can add up to real savings.

Note the price and pack size from each store, then enter them into PriceCheck to see who wins.
06

Promotions can mask bad value

"Buy 2 Get 1 Free" sounds great — but only if the base price is competitive. Calculate the effective per-unit price of the bundle and compare it against the competition. Sometimes the sale price of a rival brand is still cheaper.

07

For perishables, bigger is not always smarter

The best per-unit value is useless if half the product gets thrown away. For fresh produce, dairy, or bread — only buy the quantity you'll realistically use before it expires. Waste cancels out any savings.

08

Stock up on non-perishables at their best unit price

For items that don't expire quickly — rice, cooking oil, canned goods, toiletries, cleaning products — it makes sense to buy more when you find an exceptional per-unit price. Set a mental "buy more" threshold and stock up when you hit it.

09

Don't mix up your units

When comparing products, make sure you're comparing like with like. 1kg vs 500g — straightforward. But 750ml vs 1L vs 1.5L requires careful calculation. The PriceCheck calculator keeps things consistent so you don't make a mistake under the bright supermarket lights.

10

Build a "price memory" for your regulars

For the 10–15 products you buy every week, memorise their best per-unit price. Over time, you'll be able to instantly spot when something is genuinely a good deal versus just being marketed as one. PriceCheck helps you establish that baseline.

Common Shopping Myths — Busted

✗ Myth

"The bulk pack is always cheaper per unit."

✓ Truth

Supermarkets sometimes price smaller packs at a better unit cost, especially during promotions. Always check.

✗ Myth

"Premium brands are higher quality, so the price is justified."

✓ Truth

Many store-brand products are made by the same manufacturers. You're often just paying for packaging and advertising.

✗ Myth

"If it's on sale, it's a great deal."

✓ Truth

A sale price is only good relative to alternatives. Another brand at full price may still be cheaper per unit.

✗ Myth

"It's too much effort to compare prices on the spot."

✓ Truth

With PriceCheck on your phone, a comparison takes under 30 seconds. Open it at the shelf.
